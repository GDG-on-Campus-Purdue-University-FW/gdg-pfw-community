# Inclusivity Guidelines

Creating an inclusive community is a core goal of GDG on Campus Purdue University Fort Wayne.

We aim to design events and activities that are accessible and welcoming to participants from different backgrounds, experience levels, and perspectives.

## Designing Beginner-Friendly Events

Many participants join our events with little or no prior experience in programming or technology.

To support beginners, we aim to:

- Provide clear explanations during talks and workshops
- Avoid assuming prior knowledge
- Encourage experienced members to help beginners
- Provide clear event instructions and expectations

## Supporting Different Participation Styles

Some participants are comfortable speaking in large groups, while others prefer smaller discussions or one-on-one interactions.

Organizers should:

- Encourage balanced participation during discussions
- Avoid allowing a small number of people to dominate conversations
- Create opportunities for quieter participants to contribute

## Accessibility Considerations

Whenever possible, we try to:

- Host events in venues that are wheelchair accessible
- Provide slides or written materials alongside presentations
- Use clear visual materials that help participants follow the content

## Financial Accessibility

We try to keep community events free whenever possible. When events require a fee, we aim to keep costs as low as possible and explore options such as sponsorships or community support to reduce financial barriers.

This is especially important in communities such as CodeDay Lucknow, where many participants are high school students who may not have the resources to attend expensive events.

## Continuous Improvement

Inclusivity is an ongoing effort. We regularly gather feedback from community members and use it to improve future events.

Our goal is to ensure that everyone who attends our events feels welcomed, respected, and supported.
